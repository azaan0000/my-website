Goat Care: Feeding, Behaviour, Housing, Common Diseases & Basic Treatment

Made for beginners and small farmers • Reading time: 10–12 minutes
Farm Animals Welfare Focused Simple English

Goats are one of the most popular farm animals because they are intelligent, friendly, easy to raise and require less space compared to cows or buffaloes. With proper care, goats can provide milk, meat, fibre and even natural weed control.

Healthy goats grow fast, breed well and resist disease better. Most health problems can be avoided through good feeding, clean housing and regular observation.

1. Understanding Goat Behaviour

Goats are curious and social animals. A healthy goat is active and alert, while sickness often shows as isolation or loss of appetite.

2. Housing and Shelter

  • Dry and raised flooring
  • Proper ventilation
  • Protection from rain and cold winds
  • Strong fencing

3. Feeding and Watering

  • Green fodder and leaves
  • Dry hay for fibre
  • Mineral mixture and salt blocks
  • Clean drinking water at all times

4. Common Diseases

  • Pneumonia
  • Diarrhoea
  • Bloat
  • Foot rot
  • Parasites

5. Prevention

Daily observation, clean housing and timely veterinary care prevent most goat diseases.
